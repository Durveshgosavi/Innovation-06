Test Your Lung Capacity

Take a deep breath and hold it for as long as you can. Time yourself and see how you improve over time! This is a simple way to measure your lung capacity and see the benefits of a healthy lifestyle.

  • 10 seconds: Good
  • 20 seconds: Great
  • 30 seconds: Excellent

Breathing Exercises

Try these simple breathing exercises to improve your lung capacity and reduce stress:

  • Diaphragmatic Breathing: Lie on your back and place one hand on your chest and the other on your stomach. Breathe in slowly through your nose, feeling your stomach rise. Breathe out slowly through your mouth, feeling your stomach fall.
  • Pursed-Lip Breathing: Breathe in slowly through your nose for two seconds. Pucker your lips as if you are about to whistle and breathe out slowly for four seconds.
